Shining Light on Darkness

AstroLink empowers citizen scientists to help fight light pollution—one star at a time.

What Is AstroLink?

AstroLink is a global citizen science project that asks people like you to go outside, look up at the night sky, and count stars in constellations like Orion or Leo. By submitting what you see, you help us track light pollution and visualize how it's changing over time.

Why Light Pollution Is a Problem

Light pollution doesn't just block our view of the stars — it affects ecosystems, wastes energy, and disrupts our natural sleep cycles. Wildlife, especially nocturnal animals, rely on natural light cycles to navigate and survive. Bright city lights can confuse migratory birds, sea turtles, and even insects.

When we lose the night sky, we lose more than stars — we lose a connection to the universe and our own biological rhythm.

How It Works

  • Go outside on a clear night away from bright city lights.
  • Let your eyes adjust to the dark for 10–15 minutes.
  • Locate a target constellation like Orion or Leo.
  • Count how many stars you can clearly see.
  • Submit your results on our site to contribute to the global light pollution map.

Our Goal

Our mission is to build a real-time, community-powered map of light pollution across the globe. The more submissions we receive, the clearer the map becomes — helping scientists, cities, and communities understand where action is needed most.

Coming Soon: Interactive Light Pollution Map

We’re developing a dynamic map that will glow brighter where the stars are fewer. Each star count you submit becomes a pixel in this larger picture, helping to track the spread of light pollution over time.

At the end of the project cycle, we’ll compile a research report summarizing our findings — showcasing how light pollution has evolved across regions and highlighting areas most in need of action.

Your data matters. Let’s reclaim the night, together.
