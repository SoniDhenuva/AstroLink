About the Program

AstroLink is a citizen science project where people like you help map light pollution by counting the stars they can see in specific constellations like Orion, Leo, and others. The fewer stars visible, the stronger the local light pollution.

The Goal

Our mission is to collect star visibility data from around the world and create an evolving light pollution map based on user submissions. This map will show how human-made light is affecting our view of the cosmos.

How It Works

  • Go outside at night and find a constellation like Orion or Leo.
  • Count how many stars you can clearly see.
  • Submit your observation to our site.
  • Your data helps build a bigger picture of night sky visibility.
